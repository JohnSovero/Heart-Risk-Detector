* Typical (classic) angina chest pain consists of (1) Substernal chest pain or discomfort that is (2) Provoked by exertion or emotional stress and (3) relieved by rest or nitroglycerine (or both).
Atypical (probable) angina chest pain applies when 2 out of 3 criteria of classic angina are present.
Asymptomatic angina is a condition of reduced oxygen-rich blood flow to the heart that occurs in the absence of chest discomfort or other symptoms of angina.
Non-anginal chest pain applies in the absence of the criteria. (References: TimeOfCare and National Library of Medicine)

** Thalassemia is a blood disorder caused when the body doesn't make enough of a protein called hemoglobin. For this application, there's three cases: Fixed defect (no blood flow in some part of the heart), reversible defect (a blood flow is observed but it's not normal) and normal blood flow. (Reference: Centers for Disease Control and Prevention)
